Snapit, a fun and simple way to manage digital assets from your mobile devices

We have taken photos, videos more today than ever in human history. Managing digital assets have not been an easy task, Snapit makes this simple.

The Story

Monica is 30 years old stay home mother with 2 beautiful daughters. She has been graphic design freelancer for the past couple years. Ever since her first daughter was born, she took advantage of her mobile phone to record all the precious moments with her family. As time went by, she found that her digital assets has grown ‘exponentially’ and has gradualy become unmanageabled; She is desperate to find a tool to help her to organize...
Jennifer is amateur journalist. She uses various mobile applications to take photos, videos, audio and note for anything that she found interesting. However, to search through her digital assets respository is always a tedious task because they are scattered in many places. She needs a better tool that is easy to use and can quickly to search for the digital assets he need…

So...

Monica and Jennifer searched through the internet try to find the right tool. As a results, they've tried Evernote, Picasa, Instangram etc; however none of them could provide a unified solution for what they looking for.

Business Opportunity

[quote: "CAN I HAVE ONE APP TO DO ALL THESE STUFFS FOR ME"]

It turns out there are many mobile phone users are facing similar problems like Monica and Jennifer. Most of the mobile applications lack of features that users are looking for. While one application can do one thing but can’t do another; therefore, people usually end up using multiple applications to store their digital assets.

Research & Discovery

To understand the opportunity better, we conducted an online survey. To build the survey, we were using a service from Typeform.com. We then using facebook and email as a communication channel to reach out to evaluate the market needs.

User Mental Model

We also conducted interviews with some potential users to understanding their motivations and thought processes.

Usability testing

We ran usability test to identify some major defects on the application, which hinder user to perform major tasks. It also serve as an opportunity to learn from test user’s opinions and suggestions.

For the first task, all 3 testers were able to find their own way to tackle the task. For second task, testers seem able to finish the first half (taking a video); however, have trouble to find out how to put it to a private journal. For the third task, testers was somehow managed to tackle it; however doesn’t know how to take the advantage of tagging feature the app provides.

[Notable issue & Fix #1]





Users was assuming they have to somehow login to the application before they can do anything. In fact, they can just click any of the buttons from the toolbar to start take video, audio, picture right away.

[Notable issue & Fix #2]





Users have difficulty to distinguish public/private journal and they were not sure how to set privacy for a journal.

[Notable issue & Fix #3]





Users could not find out where is the global search and not sure how to use the tag to make search easy and efficient
